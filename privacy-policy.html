Privacy Policy

How Bookworm handles your data — in plain language, and as briefly as honestly possible.

Introduction

This document explains how Bookworm — a reading application for Android — handles your information. It is intentionally short and written in plain language. The same content is also available inside the application itself.

The single most important thing to know: Bookworm is designed to operate entirely on your device. Your books, your reading progress, and the time you spend reading never need to leave your device for the application to function.

Local data and storage

Your library is stored entirely on your device. This includes the books you have added, your reading position in each one, the cumulative time you have spent reading, and your display preferences. All of this information resides in the application's private storage on your phone.

Bookworm does not operate any server that receives this data. There is no cloud synchronisation, no online account system, and no infrastructure capable of storing personal information about your reading habits. The application contains no code to communicate with any such system.

If you uninstall Bookworm or clear its data through your device settings, your library and reading history are removed with it. No backups are retained, and no data can be recovered after deletion.

Network access

Bookworm uses the internet for a single purpose: retrieving cover artwork for the books in your library.

When you add a new book, the application sends basic, non-personal book information — typically the title and, where available, the author — to publicly available catalog services in order to locate a matching cover image. These requests contain no user identifiers, no account details, no device fingerprints, and no information about how or when you read. They are not used for tracking, profiling, or any form of analytics.

Once a cover has been retrieved, it is cached locally so the same request is not made again. If your device is offline, or you choose to restrict the application's network access, Bookworm continues to function normally; only cover images are replaced with placeholders.

What we do not collect

Bookworm contains no analytics frameworks, no advertising libraries, no crash reporters that transmit data externally, and no telemetry systems of any kind. These are not disabled behind a setting — they are not present in the application at all.

As a direct consequence, the application does not know which books you open, how long you spend reading, what time of day you read, what device you are using, what region you are in, or how frequently you return. It does not assign user identifiers, because there are none to assign. There are no cookies, no fingerprinting, and no background processes that contact external services.

In summary, there is no mechanism through which personal information could leave your device — because no such mechanism was built.

Permissions

Bookworm declares only the Android permissions it actively requires.

Internet access is used solely to fetch book cover images from public catalog services, as described in the Network access section. It is not used for analytics, communication with any private server, or data collection of any kind.

Opening books does not require a storage permission. Books are selected through Android's system file picker, and the application receives only the specific file you choose. The same applies when opening a book from a file manager or a share intent — Bookworm has access only to the files you have explicitly provided.

The application does not request access to your contacts, location, microphone, camera, messages, call logs, accounts, or any background activity. None of these are necessary to read a book.

Cover artwork

Cover images displayed alongside your books are retrieved from publicly available catalog services. They are used solely to help you identify books within your personal library on your own device.

Bookworm does not claim ownership of any cover artwork. All rights remain with the publishers, authors, and catalog providers from which the images originate. The artwork is never redistributed, repackaged, or used in any commercial or advertising context — Bookworm contains no advertising of any kind.

Attribution

Some cover artwork displayed in Bookworm is provided by Apple, through the Apple Books and iTunes catalog. These images are retrieved solely to identify books within your personal library, and all rights to that artwork remain with Apple and the original copyright holders.

Apple Books is a trademark of Apple Inc.

Our approach

Bookworm is built and maintained by an independent developer and is currently available for Android phones only. Its feature set is intentionally small and focused on the act of reading.

The decision not to include accounts, cloud synchronisation, or analytics is deliberate. Each of these would have required additional infrastructure, additional places where user data could reside, and additional permissions or sign-ups — none of which are necessary for the application to do its job. Keeping Bookworm local-only allows it to remain simple, transparent, and easy to verify.

Contact

Questions, concerns, and feedback regarding this policy or the application itself can be sent through the contact options listed on the Google Play Store page for Bookworm.

Because Bookworm has no user accounts and no in-app messaging, the Google Play Store listing is the primary channel for inbound communication. Any future revisions to this policy will be published at this same URL and reflected within the application itself.
